Advantages

This way of logging in has the following benefits.

  • Obviously, there are no passwords any more for sites that adopt SLWP.
    So, there are no dozens of them for you to remember.
  • No centrally stored passwords that can be stolen by the millions, including yours.
    Instead, the keys that are centrally stored are useless for logging in from a client.
  • The website needs to prove it can decrypt your encrypted random number.
    A phishing site is unable to do so, thus eliminating this threat.
  • It is a two-factor authentication method by design and easily upgraded to multi-factor authentication.
    A combination of something you know, and/or something you have, and/or something you are.
    Just add hardware tokens and biometrics for one or more persons to make it multi-factor.
  • Logging in can be fully automated.
    Just hit the login button on the website and you're in.
  • You can pick just any username you want for any and all accounts you have; no clashes with other people's accounts.
    "That account is already taken" is a thing from the past.
  • Accounts cannot be linked by colluding websites to the same person.
    Your anonimity is protected, unless you leave your name...
  • Login keys are automatically and periodically changed.
    You are not aware of rekeying but will enhance security.

Disadvantages

Surely, there are disadvantages.

  • You have to have your keys, otherwise you are not able to login.
    Not in a million years.
